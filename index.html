    About Us

    AEC Hardware Club is a community-driven initiative that focuses on inspiring and empowering students interested in hardware and Software engineering, electronics, and technology. Through hands-on workshops, projects, and events, the club provides members with opportunitiesto learn about hardware design, prototyping, and IoT applications.

    • The Club promotes collaboration in hardware development and engineering.
    • We provide practical experience through projects, workshops, and labs.
    • Members work together on innovative projects and competitions.
    • Faculty and industry mentors offer valuable insights and support.
    • We share our passion for hardware through outreach and educational events.

    Rules & Regulations

    Rules & Regulations

    Welcome to theAEC Hardware ClubEvent - IHMMC Hardware Model Making Competition! This event encourages creativity, innovation, and teamwork as participants design and construct models showcasing their technical skills. To ensure a fair and enjoyable experience for everyone, please adhere to the following rules and regulations:

    • Open to all college students. Teams can consist of 4 to 6 members.
    • All participants must register online before the deadline. Late entries will not be accepted.
    • Models should align with the specified theme, which will be announced prior to the competition.
    • Participants can use any materials except for pre-made kits or fully assembled models. All materials must be sourced independently.
    • All models must be original creations. Plagiarism or copying from existing designs will lead to disqualification.
    • Teams must prepare a brief presentation (maximum 5 minutes) to explain their model's functionality and design process.
    • Models will be evaluated based on creativity, functionality, aesthetics, and presentation. Detailed criteria will be provided.
    • Participants must follow safety guidelines when working with tools and materials. The organizing committee will not be responsible for any injuries.
    • Any behavior deemed unsportsmanlike or violations of the rules may result in disqualification.
    • Completed models must be submitted by [insert submission date] for judging.
    • After the competition, teams will receive constructive feedback from judges to enhance their future projects.
    • Prizes will be awarded for 1st, 2nd, and 3rd places, along with special awards for categories such as "Most Innovative" and "Best Presentation."
